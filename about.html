I can't remember any point in my life where I wasn't distinctively into drawing. That, reading, and playing flash games were pretty much all I did when I was really little.

At age 9, I started messing around on kids' programming website Scratch, publishing my work under the username Crafty190. From that point on, coding joined art on my roster of brain chemistry altering interests.

Before I knew it, I went from making Warrior Cats RPGs in blocks to dabbling in stuff like assembly language, and now I'm studying computer science in college.

Despite all this, I'm honestly not sure what I want to do in life. I like what I'm learning, but I don't want to light another match and toss it into the cesspool that is modern technology. Maybe I'll become a hacker or something.

Anyways, this website is named "Project C190" after "Crafty190", and most of the graphics are drawn with a mouse on Scratch as a tribute to the early days.
